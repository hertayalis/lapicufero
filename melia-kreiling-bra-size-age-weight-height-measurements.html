Biography:

Melia Kreiling was born on 6 October 1990 in Geneva, Switzerland. She earned her acting training from the London School of Dramatic Art. In 2012, she made her film debut in Suspension of Disbelief. That same year, she had her first TV role in an episode of Rosamunde Pilcher. She later played the role of Bianca in some episodes of The Borgias.

In 2013, she portrayed Bathsheba in the miniseries The Bible. That same year, she starred in the direct-video movie Company of Heroes. After this, she appeared as Bereet in the superhero film Guardians of the Galaxy. She went on to play notable roles in such films as MindGamers and The Last Note. Kreiling also became known for playing Daliyah Al-Yazbek in Tyrant, Hannah Taub in The Last Tycoon, and Alycia Vrettou in Salvation.
