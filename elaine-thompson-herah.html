Elaine Thompson-Herah is a Jamaican sprinter who specializes in the 100 and 200 meter dashes. She won gold in both races at the 2016 Rio Olympics, and she also took silver in the 4×100 meter relay. She also won gold in the 4×100 m relay and silver in the 200 m at the 2015 World Championships. Her breakthrough came in 2015, when she ran the 100 meters in under 11 seconds for the first time and set a personal best of 21.66 seconds in the 200 meters.

She was the Pan American Games champion in 2019 and has also won the Diamond League thrice. With her current second fastest time of 10.61 seconds in women’s history, she won gold in the 100 m at the 2020 Tokyo Olympics, becoming the fastest woman alive. She is currently the sixth-fastest woman in history in the 200 meter dash. Elaine is currently training with Stephen Francis, who is also the former coach of Shelly-Ann Fraser-Pry, a legendary athlete and Elaine’s compatriot. ‘Sprint Queen’ is her moniker.

What is Elaine Thompson-Herah Nationality?

Elaine Thompson-Herah went to Christiana High School and then Manchester High School for her education. She was a good but not excellent scholastic sprinter during her high school years. She was kicked off the track team in 2011, her senior year at Manchester High, for disciplinary grounds.
