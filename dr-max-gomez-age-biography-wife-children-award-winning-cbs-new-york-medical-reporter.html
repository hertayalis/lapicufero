Biography

Dr. Max Gomez’s journey began in Cuba, where he was born before his family emigrated to Miami, Florida during his early years. He pursued a path of academic excellence, graduating cum laude from Princeton University and earning a Ph.D. from the Wake Forest University School of Medicine. Throughout his illustrious career, Dr. Max Gomez showcased his expertise and commitment to medical journalism across various esteemed networks. Notable positions included WNBC-TV, WNEW-TV, KYW-TV, Fox 5 New York, NBC 4 New York, and CBS New York. His exceptional reporting and insightful storytelling garnered widespread recognition, earning him multiple New York Emmy Awards, Philadelphia Emmys, and a UPI honor for Best Documentary on AIDS.

Dr. Gomez’s impact extended far beyond the realm of journalism, as he was actively involved in numerous advisory boards and organizations. His efforts brought him face-to-face with presidents and popes, further solidifying his reputation as a trusted source in the health and science sectors. Additionally, he co-authored three influential books on health and science, leaving a lasting legacy in the field. During the challenging times of the COVID-19 pandemic, Dr. Max Gomez emerged as a beacon of wisdom and compassion. Known for his steady voice of reason, he provided invaluable guidance and support to viewers, demonstrating his genuine care for the welfare of others. His dedication to delivering accurate information during the crisis solidified his position as a trusted authority and a source of comfort for many.

Wife and Children

On Saturday, September 3, 2023, the world bid farewell to a true icon in medical journalism. Dr. Max Gomez, aged 72, passed away after a prolonged illness, leaving behind his loving wife, Dr. Denise C. Gomez, and their two children, Max Jr. and Christina. Although he may no longer be with us, his contributions to journalism, his unwavering dedication to the public, and his ability to connect with viewers will forever be remembered. Dr. Max Gomez’s legacy will continue to inspire future generations and serve as a testament to the impact one individual can make in the world.
